TOPIC: UTF-8

Behold, my contribution notes on Text Editors.

My understanding of UTF-8 is that it is the reason that we're capable of placing symbols within text without having to render them as individual objects, rather utilizing the native fonts and characters within. What stood out the most to me was the potential miscommunication that can occur thanks to older models' incompatibility with the new system. While taking Visual Communication we've gone over the basics of communication, and where the miscommunication/noise comes in is here within the encoding and decoding process. Older machines won't decode the bytes correctly, leading to the mojibake problem. In order for the message to be received properly, it needs to be encoded in a way that the receiver can decode it easily, and if the system is working on a different cipher, the message is distorted. UTF-8 is the workaround that allows the characters, and thus language and message, be preserved, enabling more ease of communication, provided both sides are capable of drawing from the UTF-8 database, which, considering UTF-8 is used in 87% of sites worldwide, is highly probable.
