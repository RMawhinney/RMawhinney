SUBJECT: Coding

In order for anything to be visible or accessible on the internet, there must be some way it is encoded. Whether it is a site or app, or private software, there must be some way it is coded. This subject would cover a broad variety topics that could in itself be an entire enormous database, but this Web Technologies course was not about deeper meanings so much as broadening horizons. To that end, I have chosen the ones that have broadened my understanding of Web Technologies the most over these past few months. The subjects covered in this section include Semantic Versioning, Text Editors, UTF - 8, Bootstrap, NPM, and Grunt.js. While this could be a location to go over what I've learned in HTML, the basics for that my notes are insufficient, and the majority of what I've learned is presented in the Bootstrap section under the Training area.
