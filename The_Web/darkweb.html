TOPIC: Darkweb

Behold, my contribution notes on the Darkweb.

The darknet, in essence, is a goliath of potential. Truly, the allure of a private, uncensored environment to pursue your interests, ideas and markets is seductive by nature. The offer for things that aren't easily accessible with the safety and familiarity of existing sites makes the darknet quite easily an attractive outlet. As encapsulated by the speaker, that's great if you care about liberty, anonymity, and democracy. But for those whose interests are objectionable recreationally, illegal rationally, or appalling inherently, the darkweb is much more than just a typical playground. The great potential for good is matched overwhelmingly by a great potential for insidiousness, one which has not and will not go untapped. Fears are well-founded that if monitoring and policing were to occur on the darknet to avoid reprehensible behavior, the very draw of the darknet as an open, uncensored environment would vanish. The anonymity versus accountability and safety versus freedom are ideals that have been fought and contemplated for centuries. Personally, I don't have any interests that would not be served by a regular, monitored internet and nothing I've pursued has been objectionable legally, though I can understand the desire for a less-regulated market. However I can't agree with any use of such an environment for pursuing illegal ventures, especially those directly harmful to other people.
