TOPIC: FTP

Behold, my contribution notes on FTP.

Near as I can understand it we've all not only seen FTP in action ourselves but we've already used it for this course. Imagine you're on a deadline, and your final draft is contained on a flash drive. You hand the flash drive off to your superior for them to publish it. FTP would be a digital example of this wherein the flash drive is all the files you wish to upload, and your superior is your hosting server/site. We've already used Google Drive sync and Gitpush, which are both in a sense FTP clients, what with us being able to add pages and documents remotely. Whereas in the physical example, one could worry about losing the flash drive, with FTP digitally that no longer becomes an issue. The only potential issue could be security, if the content can be in any way compromised or sensitive information could be stolen. Of course that risk is still incredibly low, same as it would be with someone physically stealing your flash drive
